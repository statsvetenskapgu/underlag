Thesis examination seminars

Completed and submited theses are discussed at final examination seminars. These seminars take place at the end of the Spring semester, if you are unable to submit at that date there will also be a submission round in September 2025 and in February 2026. The May and September seminars are held on campus while the February seminars can be held online.

You are welcome to attend the examination seminars from previous cohorts held in February 2025. They will count toward the total numbers of seminars you are required to attend, and you will give you an idea of what to expect when you submit your own thesis!

Entry requirements

Please note that there are entry requirements for the Master's Thesis, for entry requirements see the Syllabus above.

To be registered on the thesis course: Completion of the two IAGG core courses (AG2110 & AG2120), one in-depth semi-elective course (15 credits), and one research methods course (15 credits). On the methods course the student must have completed all assignments except the final paper, but must have submitted the final paper. If you are conditionally admitted you will have to contact the course administrator to be registered if you meet the entry requirements.

 

Course evaluation

We ask you to please fill in the course evaluation at the end of your course. You'll find it in the course Cavas page menu, under "Course Evaluations". The evaluations help us develop and improve our courses and your contribution is valuable! 

You can read reports on previous cohorts of this course and others, in the publicly available Canvas course "Kursrapporter/Course Reports". Note that you may have to look under both of the modules "Master's Programme in Political Science..." and "Master's Programme in International Administration and Global Governance" to find your course, as some courses are included in several programmes.
